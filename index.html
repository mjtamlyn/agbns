What is the National Series?

The Archery GB National series is the premier domestic archery competition. There are 5 legs through the year, with the top 4 ranked archers in each category progressing to the finals. The competition is open to all, shooting both compound and recurve bows.

The 2012 edition has just 4 legs to make way for the Olympics, with the overall rankings determined by the best 2 results. Oxford Archers were awarded the first leg in May, comprising a full weekend of shooting, a FITA 720 with head to heads on the Saturday, followed by a full FITA round on the Sunday. The second leg was the Midsummer Tournament, held at the National Sports Centre in Lilleshall, with the shooting following the same format as the first leg. The final two legs were hosted by Clophill Archery Club in Bedfordshire, and take place over one weekend. Both days are a FITA 720 followed by head to heads.

The final returns to Oxford, this time in the city centre on Christ Church Meadows. Head to head matches comprising semi finals and finals take place in each category. Taking place over the August bank holiday weekend, between the Olympics and the Paralympics, the finals are sure to be a spectacular occasion.

Oxford 12th & 13th May 2012

The first leg of the 2012 National Series took place in Oxford on the 12th and 13th of May. Nearly 200 archers took part over the weekend in dry but breezy conditions. Three of new newly announced Olympic teams were in attendence, along with both of the reserve archers.

Soon to be 6 type olympian Alison Williamson was in fine form, winning both the ranking round and the FITA, and finishing second in the Head to Heads to follow olympian Naomi Folkard to take victory in the ladies recurve. Olympic reserve Charlotte Burgess was in third, with Jeanetta Braun in 4th. The men's olympic reserve archer Michael Peart also shone, seeding second and then winning the head to heads, with a 5th place in the FITA round proving enough for victory, ahead of Jon Shales and last year's overall winner Simon Terry. Bob Lowe wrapped up the top four.

A strong field was also present in the compound division, including all four of the mens finalists from the 2011. They did not disappoint, with Duncan Busby dominant in all but the final of the head to heads, where a national record of 149 out of 150 from Adam Ravenscroft saw Busby pushed into second place. Ravenscroft, Grimwood and White completed the top four on a successful day for the big names. The ladies was won by Andrea Gales after victory in both the 720 and the FITA. Rikki Bingham took third, and Emma Terry matched her husband with a third place after two second places on the first day. Pauline Burfitt's consistent good performances earned her fourth, ahead of Christie Westman who although winning the elimination, was 8th in the ranking and 7th in the FITA to drop to 5th overall.

Lilleshall 30th June & 1st July 2012

For the second leg, we moved to the home of Archery GB - the national sports centre at Lilleshall. The regular midsummer shoot took on the same format as the Oxford leg, with a 720 and head to heads followed by a FITA. The difficult conditions continued, with the archers contending with heavy showers on Saturday and windy conditions throughout.

In the compound division, Dani Brown was in spectacular form as she dominated all three competitions to wrap up victory. Andrea Gales carried on her good momentum from Oxford with a 3rd in the ranking round followed by two second places to take second. Pauline Burfitt finished in third and Nichola Simpson in fourth. Adam Ravenscroft took the overall title this time, with three of the four big names in the top four again. Phil Glover joined them in third, enjoying a good run of form following medals at the European Championships in Cyprus. Duncan Busby missed out down in 6th after an early exit from the head the heads meaning winning the ranking round and coming second in the FITA was not enough.

Larry Godfrey returned from the World Cup in Ogden in imperious form, matching Dani's achievement in winning all three sections for a comfortable victory. The rest of the olympic team rounded out the top three, with Alan Wills pipping Simon Terry to second by virtue of a better performance in the Head to Heads. Behind them the younger generation are flexing their muscles, with Tom Barber in 4th and George Harding enjoying a fantastic run to the final of the Head to Heads to finish 5th. The Danish and British women's teams enjoyed an exciting tussle on the Saturday, with the Danish coming out on top with a 1-2 in the Head to Heads. However the top four seeds and semi finalists did not shoot the Sunday, which left the overall results open to the chasing pack. Olympic reserve Charlotte Burgess took the win, with Jeanetta Braun, Victoria Savage and Estelle Edwick rounding out the top 4.

Clophill (1) 14th July 2012

The third and fourth legs were hosted by Clophill Archery Club, in Ampthill, Bedfordshire. The first day saw some horrible weather, with heavy rain leaving parts of the field rather boggy, but the archery stayed exciting despite the weather!

In the ladies recurve, the olympians dominated, with Alison edging Naomi out to the top ranking by 3 points, but more than 40 ahead of the third placed archer. In the final Naomi reversed the result to win 6-2. Youngster Rebecca Martin finished third, with Antje Frotscher in 4th. The gents recurves also were led out by the Olympians, with Simon Terry taking first and Larry Godfrey second in the rankings. But for the second leg in a row George Harding galloped through the Head to Head matches to win, beating Simon 6-0 in the final. Larry took bronze, beating Stuart Barby.

The Ladies Compound qualification was close, with just 11 points separating the top 7. Not suprising then that the finalists were the 5th and 6th seeds, with that order being kept as Pauline Burfitt edged out Rikki Bingham 147-145. The top two seeds competed for bronze, with top seed Georgie Brown missing out to Andrea Gales 140-142. In the Gents, Chris Bell won the final on a shoot off, beating Adam Ravenscroft into second. Duncan Busby and Liam Grimwood completed the top four, with those archers all finishing according to seed.

Clophill (2) 15th July 2012

The weather thankfully improved on the Sunday, as the only a few archers arrived with places confirmed in the final. Even then, there were placings left to play for.

In the compounds, Adam Ravenscroft was not to be beaten again, recording his second win of the series with victory over Andrew Jackson in the final. Adam qualified for the series final with 2 leg wins, the only archer to do so. In the Bronze match, Colin Geenes missed out to Chris Bell, whose third place was enough to qualify for the final and push last years winner Chris White out. On the ladies, the fourth place in the standings came down to the final match, with Christie Westman beating Nichola Simpson to qualify. Andrea Gales won the bronze, beating 15th seed Anna King comfortably.

In the Gents Recurve, George Harding continued his great form in head to head matches to again reach the final, this time missing out to Tom Barber. It was enough for George to qualify for the final in first place, and for Barber to steal third place in the rankings from Simon Terry. Terry again finished third, this time beating Rob Hallworth in the bronze match to finish with four podium finishes but no victories this year. Youth beat experience in the ladies as well, with Rebecca Martin beating Naomi Folkard 6-4 in the final to secure a place at the final. 3rd of Victoria Savage was only enough to rise to 6th in the overall standings as she beat Nicky Hunt in the bronze match.
